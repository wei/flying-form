# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

**Primary:** Enterprise operators (front-desk and facilities staff) who photograph paper forms, publish them as QR-based mobile forms, and review submissions on a dashboard.

**Secondary:** End users (visitors, contractors) who scan a QR code on their own phone and complete the form via a grouped-screen wizard.

**Tertiary audience (demo context):** Hackathon judges evaluating production shape, user experience, and the ai& / Kimi-on-Japan-inference thesis.

## Product Purpose

Turn a paper form into a working, validated, bilingual mobile form in under a minute, with no manual field setup. The user fills it on their phone; the enterprise scans the resulting success QR to open the exact submission. Accessibility is a headline benefit: the same paper form that excluded non-Japanese readers, screen-reader users, and people with motor barriers becomes usable by more people.

## Positioning

Photograph a paper form; get a working, validated mobile form in seconds.

## Brand Personality

Precise, trustworthy, effortless. The interface should feel like a professional enterprise tool that quietly removes friction — crisp controls, clear hierarchy, no flourish that does not serve the task.

## Anti-references

- Not a consumer-social app: avoid bubbly cards, emoji-heavy UI, playful illustrations, and overly rounded surfaces.
- Not a generic Bootstrap/Tailwind gray-and-blue default admin: avoid muted gray text on off-white, generic card grids, and stock component styling.
- Not a dense legacy enterprise data-grid tool like old SAP/Salesforce: avoid information density for density's sake, clipped tables, and overwhelming chrome.
- Not a marketing landing page: avoid hero metrics, gradient text, and conversion theatrics.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The form builder itself should model the accessibility it promises: labeled controls, visible focus, adequate contrast, and a sensible screen-reader order.
2. **Show the state, don't explain it.** Progress, validation, QR status, and submission identity should be visible at a glance; operators should never wonder what is happening.
3. **One task per surface.** Admin creation, mobile fill, and submission review each get a clear, single-minded view rather than a kitchen-sink dashboard.
4. **Trust through precision.** Widgets, spacing, alignment, and copy should feel intentional and engineered — not generated or default.
5. **Phone-first for the fill, desk-friendly for the admin.** The two primary contexts have different constraints; optimize each without forcing a single layout on both.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA for contrast, focus indication, touch target size, semantic labels, and keyboard operability. Reduced motion should be honored. Bilingual English/Japanese labels are a core feature, not an add-on.
