# Flying Form — 2-Minute Demo Plan

A live, single happy-path demo built to score on all five ai& criteria. Scenario: **visitor check-in at a Tokyu Land building** (the front-desk "scan your QR back" handshake fits the room). One transformation moment, value landed by ~0:40. **Fully presenter-driven: no handing the phone to a judge, no waiting on the room. Every second is scripted and controlled.**

> Criteria are subject to change and may be updated by the ai& team. Re-map the beats below if the rubric shifts.

## The one-sentence pitch (say this first, before touching anything)

> "This is a Japanese paper form. In the next two minutes I'll turn it into a working, bilingual mobile form with one photo, fill it on a phone, and scan the receipt back at the front desk. Every bit of AI runs on Kimi K2.7, on ai&, in Japan."

## How each criterion is earned

**01 · Innovation — the fresh angle is *safety by construction*, not OCR.**
Anyone can OCR a form. Flying Form's generator is physically incapable of emitting an invalid field: Kimi's output is constrained to a closed nine-type vocabulary and Zod-validated before render, retried on failure, never shown broken. Say the line out loud: *"a bad photo can't produce a broken form, because the generator has no vocabulary for one."* Pair it with the sovereignty framing (AI in Japan) and accessibility-as-a-byproduct. That trio is the "unique, meaningful use of AI."

**02 · Technical execution — show the round trip completing live.**
The proof is the loop closing on stage without a patch: photo → schema → QR → phone wizard → submit → success QR → scanned back → submission opens. Mention the moving parts once, quickly: vision + text on Kimi, a CORS proxy Cloud Function, Firestore live listeners, client-side QR generate *and* scan. Do not narrate architecture; let the working loop carry it.

**03 · Impact — name the person and the daily moment.**
Front-desk and facilities staff re-key forms every single day; a foreign visitor or a screen-reader user can't use the paper at all. This is a felt, same-day problem for a room of enterprise judges. The English toggle is the single most concrete inclusion win, and it happens live in front of them.

**04 · Presentation — one story, one path, fully under your control.**
Physical prop → transformation → the form filled on a mirrored phone → the receipt scanned back at the desk. No handoff to the room: handing a judge a phone burns time and invites a fumble you can't script around. Mirror the phone to the projector so everyone sees the fill and the success QR. One happy path, no mode-switching. Rehearse the exact taps until the toggle and the scan are muscle memory.

**05 · Use of ai& — make the dependency explicit and load-bearing.**
Vision (photo → schema) is the thing that could not happen without the model, and it runs on Kimi K2.7 on ai& inference in Japan. Say "on ai&" at the value beat *and* at the close. The sovereignty point (PII processed on Japan-based inference, not shipped to a foreign API) is the thoughtful integration that adds real value, not a checkbox call.

## Pre-flight checklist

- [ ] **Pick and print the paper form** (use a real, slightly messy one from `sample-forms/`). Have a physical copy in hand.
- [ ] **Pre-cache one known-good schema** for that exact form as a lag fallback. If the live call is slow, fall back and **say so** ("this one's pre-generated so we don't watch a spinner"). Honest disclosure protects the technical score.
- [ ] **Warm the function** with one throwaway call right before you present (cold starts on the proxy add seconds).
- [ ] **Set up screen mirroring** for the presenter phone so the room sees the fill and the success QR. Test it on the actual projector before the slot.
- [ ] **Charged presenter phone** on the form URL, plus the demo laptop on the dashboard.
- [ ] **Rehearse the JA/EN toggle and the success-QR scan twice.** These are the two beats most likely to fumble.
- [ ] **Network fallback:** phone hotspot ready; the room wifi will be contested.
- [ ] **Have the submissions table already open** on the dashboard so the scanned submission lands somewhere visible.

## If something breaks

- **Schema call slow or fails** → drop to the pre-cached schema, disclose it, keep moving. Do not debug on stage.
- **Mirroring drops** → keep narrating and hold the phone up to the room; the beats still read. Reconnect between beats, not mid-sentence.
- **Scanner won't read the success QR** → the dashboard also lists submissions live; tap the newest row to open the same view. Same outcome, no dead air.
